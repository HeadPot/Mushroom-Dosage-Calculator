FAQ

Is psilocybin safe if I have a history of mental illness?
This is the most important safety question. Psilocybin is contraindicated for anyone with a personal or family history of schizophrenia, schizoaffective disorder, bipolar I, or any psychosis-spectrum condition — every major clinical research programme, including Johns Hopkins and Imperial College London, excludes these individuals from trials for this reason. The risk is not physical toxicity but triggering a latent psychotic episode that may not resolve. Active severe depression or suicidal ideation are also exclusion criteria in all published research. If any of this applies to you or a first-degree relative, psilocybin is not a suitable substance regardless of dose.
What's the difference between a difficult experience and a bad trip?
A difficult experience still has a frame around it — challenging emotions, confrontational thoughts, discomfort — but you remain grounded enough to know what you took and that it will end. A bad trip involves losing that anchor entirely: real terror, paranoia, or ego dissolution with no sense of ground. Dose is a factor, but set and setting usually matter more. Most difficult experiences resolve positively if you surrender rather than fight them. If someone is in genuine distress: calm voice, safe space, constant presence, no new stimulation. Never leave them alone.
It's been 90 minutes and I barely feel anything — should I redose?
Almost certainly not yet. On a full stomach, onset can push past 2 hours. Redosing too early is one of the most common causes of overwhelming experiences — the first dose and the second peak simultaneously, far stronger than intended. Wait the full 2 hours before considering it. If genuinely nothing at 2.5 hours on an empty stomach, a half-dose top-up is reasonable. Never take a second full dose.
How does tolerance work, and how long to reset?
Psilocybin tolerance builds after a single dose via rapid down-regulation of 5-HT₂A serotonin receptors. Dose again within 2–3 days and the effect is noticeably blunted. Full reset takes approximately 2–4 weeks. Cross-tolerance exists with LSD, DMT, and other classic psychedelics — all acting on the same receptors. This is why recreational weekend use produces rapidly diminishing returns, and why every microdosing protocol includes multiple off-days. Tolerance is not dependence — psilocybin produces no physical withdrawal and has low addiction potential.
Can you combine mushrooms with cannabis?
Cannabis amplifies psilocybin for most people — sometimes significantly. Many unexpectedly intense experiences involve cannabis added mid-trip when things seemed manageable. Whether this is desirable or overwhelming depends entirely on dose, tolerance, and setting. If you do combine, use it sparingly, only after the peak, and only if you already have experience with both substances separately.
Why did I feel almost nothing at this dose last time?
Three most common reasons: tolerance — dose within the past 2–3 weeks means you need significantly more for the same effect; food — a full stomach can reduce absorption dramatically, sometimes turning a 2g experience into a barely perceptible one; batch variation — potency varies 30–50% between flushes of the same strain from the same grower. If the number barely touched you, try 20–30% higher next time — not double.
Should I fast beforehand?
Fast for at least 3–4 hours for reliable onset and full effect. A full stomach doesn't make the experience safer — it makes it slower, less predictable, and often significantly weaker. If nausea is a concern, ginger tea beforehand helps without blunting the experience. Fatty meals slow gastric emptying the most — avoid them in the hours before.
How long does it last?
The full experience typically runs 4–6 hours, with the peak lasting 2–3 hours. Heroic doses can extend to 6–8 hours. Come-up is 30–90 minutes depending on stomach content, or 15–30 minutes with Lemon Tek. An afterglow — tired but often reflective — can persist several hours beyond that. Plan the full day and clear the next morning for anything above Level 3.
My strain isn't in the list — what do I use?
Marketing names for cubensis strains multiply faster than anyone can track — many are rebranded genetics, often with inflated potency claims. Use Golden Teacher as a conservative baseline for any unknown cubensis, or Penis Envy if it's marketed as a PE variant. For anything described as "extremely potent" or a new hybrid, start at 50% of the suggested dose until you know the batch.
Fresh vs dried — does this calculator work for fresh mushrooms?
No — dried only. Fresh mushrooms are approximately 90% water, so the rough conversion is 10:1 by weight (15g fresh ≈ 1.5g dried). Potency also varies more in fresh mushrooms because water content fluctuates with age and handling. Dry to cracker-crisp consistency before weighing for the most accurate dosing.
What's the difference between cultivated strains and wild species?
Every cultivated "strain" — Golden Teacher, PE, B+, Enigma — is Psilocybe cubensis. Wild species like P. azurescens, P. cyanescens, P. semilanceata, and Panaeolus cyanescens are entirely different organisms. Most wild species are meaningfully more potent than cubensis and vary more with season, habitat, and drying method. The calculator adjusts per species, but treat wild species with additional caution regardless.
Are my mushrooms still good?
For safety: discard anything with mold (white or green fuzz), a musty or sour smell, or visible slime — these indicate contamination regardless of storage. For potency: bone-dry mushrooms in an airtight jar with a desiccant packet, stored in a cool dark place, retain most of their potency for 1–2 years. Blue bruising when broken is a good sign — psilocin is still present and oxidising. Soft or bendy mushrooms may still be fine if the jar is sealed, but re-dry them before use.
Can you take psilocybin if you're on antidepressants?
SSRIs and SNRIs blunt or completely block psilocybin effects in most people — both act on serotonin receptors, and chronic SSRI use down-regulates the 5-HT₂A receptors that psilocybin activates. Many people on SSRIs report needing 2–3× their expected dose for any effect, or nothing at all. Do not stop or taper SSRIs to improve a trip without medical supervision — discontinuation syndrome is real and dangerous. MAOIs are a separate and serious risk: combining with psilocybin can cause serotonin syndrome, which is a medical emergency.
Can you overdose on psilocybin?
Not in the traditional lethal sense — psilocybin has an extraordinarily high safety margin and there are no confirmed human deaths from psilocybin toxicity alone. The real risks are behavioural: at very high doses, loss of coordination and judgment can lead to accidents. Psychological overdose — an overwhelmingly intense experience with full ego dissolution, terror, and loss of reality anchoring — is very possible and can have lasting effects. This is why dose matters, why a sitter matters at high doses, and why "it's non-toxic" is not the same as "it's safe at any dose."
What is ego death and should I be trying to achieve it?
Ego dissolution — often called ego death — is the temporary loss of the sense of being a separate self. The boundary between "you" and everything else dissolves. At its best it's described as profoundly liberating, oceanic, or mystical. At its worst it's terrifying, because the part of you that knows "this will end" is the same part that disappears. It typically requires a heroic dose (4.5g+ at baseline cubensis) and an appropriate setting. It is not a goal to chase casually — it's something to approach with preparation, intention, and respect. The clinical research showing psilocybin's most profound therapeutic effects also involves the most intense experiences.
How do I trip sit for someone?
Your job as a sitter is to be a calm, stable presence — not a guide, not a therapist, not entertainment. Stay sober. Keep the environment safe, quiet, and familiar. Don't offer unsolicited reassurance or try to redirect the experience — just be there. If they become distressed: lower your voice, get on their level physically, remind them gently what they took and that it will pass. Don't restrain, don't argue with their reality, don't leave them alone. Have water and a blanket available. Know in advance whether they want to be touched or talked to during difficult moments — ask before they dose, not during.
What is wood lover's paralysis?
A rare phenomenon reported almost exclusively with wood-loving Psilocybe species — particularly P. azurescens, P. cyanescens, and P. subaeruginosa. Some users experience temporary weakness or paralysis of the legs, typically during the come-up, lasting 30 minutes to a few hours. The cause is unknown — it may be related to aeruginascin or other compounds these species produce alongside psilocybin. It resolves on its own and leaves no permanent effects, but it is alarming if unexpected. If you're using a wood-loving species, be aware, don't be alone, and don't plan to walk anywhere during the experience.
What microdosing protocol should I follow?
The most widely used is the Fadiman protocol: dose one day, off two days, repeat. This prevents tolerance build-up while allowing observation of effects over time. A typical starting microdose is 0.05–0.1g dried cubensis — you should feel nothing perceptible, just subtle shifts in mood, focus, or creativity. Stamets protocol is more aggressive: four days on, three days off. Start lower than you think you need. If you feel obviously altered, the dose is too high — a microdose should be sub-perceptual. Keep a journal; effects often accumulate and are easier to see in retrospect than in the moment.
How much stronger is Enigma than regular cubensis?
Significantly. Enigma tested at 3.82% total tryptamines at the 2021 Hyphae Cup — the highest ever recorded for any cubensis sample in a verified competition. That's roughly 3–4× average cubensis. A dose that would give a normal experience with Golden Teacher could be overwhelming with Enigma. It also can't be grown from spores — it's a sporeless mutation that only propagates via agar — so any Enigma you encounter comes from a clone chain, and potency can vary between them. Always start at a fraction of your normal dose.
How much stronger is Panaeolus cyanescens than P. cubensis?
Panaeolus cyanescens (Blue Meanie, Natal Blue Meanie) is typically 2–3× stronger than average P. cubensis by dry weight. Because they're also much smaller mushrooms, visual comparisons are extremely misleading — a handful of Pan. cyan that looks like a modest amount can be an intense experience. They also have a reputation for a faster, sharper onset. Treat them as an entirely different category from cubensis: halve your expected dose, go somewhere safe, and don't underestimate them based on appearance.

FAQ

Why did I feel almost nothing at this dose last time?
Three most likely reasons: tolerance — psilocybin tolerance spikes immediately after a dose and takes 2–3 weeks to fully reset, so if you dosed recently you'll need significantly more for the same effect; food — a full stomach slows absorption dramatically, sometimes turning a 2 g experience into a 0.5 g one; batch variation — even the same strain from the same grower can vary 30–50% in potency between flushes. If the calculator gave a number that barely touched you, try going 20–30% higher next time, not double.
Should I eat before or fast?
Fast for at least 3–4 hours before dosing if you want reliable onset and full effect. A full stomach doesn't make the experience safer — it just makes it slower, more unpredictable, and often weaker. If nausea is a concern, a very small amount of ginger tea beforehand helps without significantly blunting the experience. Avoid fatty meals in the hours before — fat slows gastric emptying more than carbs or protein.
It's been 90 minutes and I barely feel anything — should I redose?
Almost certainly not yet. Slow onset on a full stomach can push first effects past 2 hours. Redosing too early is one of the most common mistakes — people take more, then the original dose and the second hit simultaneously, often resulting in a much more intense experience than intended. Wait the full 2 hours minimum. If still nothing at 2.5 hours on a genuinely empty stomach, a half-dose redose is reasonable. Never redose a full amount.
How long does it actually last?
Peak effects typically last 2–3 hours, with the full experience running 4–6 hours for most people. Higher doses extend this — a heroic dose can run 6–8 hours. The come-up takes 30–90 minutes depending on stomach content and whether you used Lemon Tek (15–30 min). After-effects — a tired but often reflective afterglow — can persist for several more hours. Plan for a full day. Don't have obligations the next morning for higher doses.
Why is my strain not in the list?
Marketing names for cubensis strains multiply faster than anyone can track — many are rebranded versions of existing genetics, often with inflated potency claims. If you can't find your strain, use Golden Teacher as a conservative baseline for any unknown cubensis, or Penis Envy as a baseline if it's marketed as a PE variant. For anything described as "extremely potent" or a "new hybrid," start at 50% of what the calculator suggests until you know the batch.
Fresh vs dried — does the calculator work for fresh mushrooms?
No — Shroomulator calculates doses for dried mushrooms only. Fresh mushrooms are approximately 90% water, so the conversion is roughly 10:1 by weight. A 1.5 g dried dose corresponds to about 15 g fresh. Fresh mushroom potency also varies more than dried because the water content fluctuates. Weigh after drying to bone-dry cracker consistency for the most accurate dosing.
What's the difference between a bad trip and just a difficult experience?
A difficult experience has challenging emotions, confrontational thoughts, or physical discomfort — but you remain grounded enough to know what's happening and that it will end. A bad trip can involve genuine terror, paranoia, or complete loss of the sense that you took a substance and it will wear off. The dose is only one factor — set and setting often matter more. Most difficult experiences resolve positively if you surrender rather than fight. If someone is in genuine distress: calm voice, safe space, reassurance that it will end, no new stimulation. Never leave them alone.
How should I store dried mushrooms?
The two enemies are moisture and UV light. Store in an airtight container (mason jar with a desiccant packet) in a cool, dark place. Properly dried and stored mushrooms lose minimal potency over 1–2 years. Vacuum sealing extends this further. Never store in a plastic bag long-term — they're not airtight. Freezing works if the mushrooms are completely bone-dry before freezing; any residual moisture will degrade them. Check periodically for softness or mold.
Why do some people feel nothing even at high doses?
Beyond the obvious suspects (tolerance, food), there's genuine individual variation in how people metabolise psilocybin. A small subset appear to be low-responders even on an empty stomach with no prior use — likely involving gut enzyme activity and serotonin receptor density. If you've genuinely had no response three times on an empty stomach with verified mushrooms, the mushrooms were probably weak. True non-responders are rare.
Can you combine mushrooms with cannabis?
Cannabis intensifies psilocybin significantly — often more than doubling the perceived strength. A lot of difficult experiences happen because someone added cannabis mid-trip when things felt manageable. The combination can be extraordinary but it removes directional control. If you combine, use cannabis sparingly and only once you're well into the experience and feel grounded. Never during the come-up.
What's the difference between P. cubensis strains and wild species?
Every cultivated "strain" — Golden Teacher, PE, B+, Enigma — is Psilocybe cubensis. Wild species like P. azurescens, P. cyanescens, P. semilanceata, and Panaeolus cyanescens are entirely different species. Most wild species are meaningfully more potent than cubensis and vary more with season, habitat, and drying. The calculator adjusts for this per-species, but wild species warrant extra caution regardless.
How do I know if my mushrooms have lost potency?
Blue bruising on intact dried mushrooms is a good sign — psilocin oxidation is still occurring, meaning the compound is present. Degraded mushrooms often lose bruising reactivity. They should be bone-dry (cracker-crisp), have no mold, and no off smell. Slightly bendy mushrooms stored in a sealed jar with a desiccant are usually fine. If they smell musty or have any white or green fuzz, discard them.
